So language functions like a game, and the rules are always changing along with different bits of meaning. There is a way to mathemetically describe the idea that the only constant is change, that the difference between ‘A’ and itself and the difference between ‘A’ and ‘B’ along with all other differences in between, are also different from themselves. This constant is known as ‘e’. The ‘∂’ symbol means difference. ‘d’ here means deferral, which is like the bit of time between ‘A’ and ‘B’. ‘r’ here means relationality, which is like the amount that ‘A’ is still connected to itself (sometimes called trace in philosophy). You can think of ‘r’ as the amount that you are still you when going from one surface to another. You can think of ‘d’ as the amount you change when you pursue another surface out of some desire. The equation is r=ke^d where k is any value that stays the same.
